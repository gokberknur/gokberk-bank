# Servicing — competitive benchmarks

How the best run servicing, so gökberk bank can match or beat them. Use this to calibrate "how good does this
have to be?" — for servicing the answer is usually "as calm and honest as the best, and clearer than all of
them." Servicing is where trust is repaired; the bar is composure under stress.

## The bar-setters

- **Monzo — disputes.** The reference for an in-app chargeback. You open the disputed transaction, tap "get
  help / something wrong", and a guided, mostly-automated form asks the right questions (many optional;
  more detail improves the odds), takes evidence, and submits to the scheme. Crucially, Monzo is **honest
  about timing** ("can take up to 12 weeks, set by Mastercard") and **keeps you informed** — refunds
  automatically when upheld, tells you the next step when not. Warm, no-blame microcopy. If our dispute flow
  is less honest about timing or less guided than Monzo, it's wrong.
- **Revolut / Intercom — support & chat.** Self-serve-first done at scale: a Help Centre handles the common
  questions, an AI/triage layer deflects ~75%, and in-app chat escalates to a human who **fixes it in one go**
  rather than passing you around. 24/7, fast resolution, no repeating yourself. Match the self-serve-first
  structure and the "don't make them repeat themselves" discipline; stay calmer and less busy.
- **DocuSign — e-sign UX.** The reference for signing with gravity *without* friction theatre. The pillars to
  steal: an explicit **consent/disclosure** step (you agree to sign electronically), a deliberate **intent**
  act, and a **certificate of completion / audit trail** — who signed, timestamps, the completed document —
  retained and retrievable. DocuSign makes a binding act feel safe and proven. Our timestamped signed copy in
  the vault is the demo's version of the certificate of completion.
- **Document vaults (best-in-class banking apps).** A single searchable home for statements, contracts, and
  certificates, with in-app viewing and one-tap download — the contrast is incumbent banks where statements
  are buried, paginated badly, or only emailed. We win by making retrieval instant and the store unified.

## Patterns worth stealing

- **Monzo's pre-filled, guided dispute** — entry from the transaction itself, the right questions per reason,
  optional-but-encouraged evidence, honest timeline, automatic refund on success.
- **Revolut's self-serve-first ladder** — article → chat → human, with smart routing and no re-explaining.
- **DocuSign's consent + audit-trail** — explicit electronic-signature consent, then a retained, timestamped,
  downloadable record of who signed what and when.
- **"Turn this chat into a ticket"** so a conversation never evaporates — history is one continuous thread.
- **Scroll/read-progress on the signing review** — a quiet marker that you've reached the end, gating the act.

## Anti-patterns to avoid (where incumbents and even neobanks fail)

- A dispute that **fakes certainty** — presenting provisional credit as a done refund, or hiding that it can
  be reversed. The first reversal destroys trust.
- **Dead-end eligibility** — "you can't dispute this" with no reason and no alternative.
- **Blame-tinged copy** — "you claim", "you say you didn't receive" — interrogating a stressed customer.
- **Enable-Sign-immediately** — letting someone sign a binding document they demonstrably didn't read.
- **Ticket black holes** — submit and silence; status that never moves; forcing the user to re-explain on
  every reply.
- **Document silos** — statements in one place, agreements in another, signed copies nowhere; pagination and
  filters that fight the user.

## The gökberk angle

Match the neobanks on guidance and speed; **beat** them on composure and honesty. Servicing is the brand's
no-blame voice at its purest: when a customer is worried about a charge or solemn about a signature, gökberk
is the calmest, clearest party in the room. Where others reassure with noise ("Your request is super
important to us!"), we state facts plainly — the timestamp, the case stage, the temporary credit, the next
step — with one earned accent on the single primary action. The differentiator isn't a feature; it's that you
always know exactly where you stand and are never made to feel at fault.
