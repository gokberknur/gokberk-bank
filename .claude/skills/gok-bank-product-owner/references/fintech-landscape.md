# The fintech & banking landscape — where the bar is

The competitive map you carry in your head. Use it to answer "is this competitive?" and "what would *they*
do here?" Each entry: what they're known for, how they make money, and where they win and leak. The point
is not to copy any of them — it's to know exactly where the bar sits before you call something good enough.

## How to read this map

Three lessons sit on top of every entry:

1. **Only ~15% of neobanks are profitable.** The ones that are (Nubank, Revolut, Starling, Monzo, SoFi) got
   there through a **lending book** — credit cards, overdrafts, loans, BNPL — monetizing deposits, not
   through feature sprawl. Engagement is easy; *depth and lifetime value* are the hard, valuable part.
2. **The primary-account prize.** Most neobanks are someone's *second* account — the travel card, the
   spending pot — while the salary still lands at an incumbent. Only ~34% of neobank customers hold a credit
   or lending product vs. ~65% at traditional banks. Whoever becomes the account people *run their life from*
   wins the relationship. The modern definition of "primary" is shifting from "sees your salary first" to
   "touches your money most often." gökberk's ambition is to win on **trust and daily habit**, not gimmicks.
3. **Reliability governs where active money lives.** People only move their real money — salary, rent,
   savings — to a bank they trust to be boring and correct. Trust is the precondition for depth. This is why
   gökberk's calm, transparent, no-surprises posture is a *growth strategy*, not just an aesthetic.

## The neobanks (consumer current-account challengers)

- **Revolut** — the breadth king. ~65M+ users, the broadest feature set in the field: multi-currency +
  FX, instant internal sends, stocks, crypto, commodities, savings vaults, budgeting, split bills, request
  links, and paid tiers bundling travel insurance, lounge access, eSIMs. Profitable (~$1.4B pre-tax) via a
  mix of interchange, subscriptions, FX, and a growing lending/credit book. **Wins:** speed, density,
  optionality, "everything app" ambition. **Leaks:** can feel busy, cluttered, and upsell-heavy; ten options
  where one would do. *gökberk's counter:* match the speed and capability, show the **right** option and tuck
  the rest under "More," stay calm where Revolut is loud.
- **N26** — the restrained one. Clean German-licensed neobank known for real-time spending insights and
  **Spaces** (sub-accounts/pots). Closest in spirit to gökberk's editorial calm. **Wins:** clarity,
  restraint, a focused account. **Leaks:** thinner breadth than Revolut; past growth-cap and compliance
  constraints. *gökberk's counter:* keep N26's calm, add more depth and a more premium finish.
- **Monzo** — everyday clarity and community. Coral card, instant spend notifications, round-ups, Pots,
  shared tabs, warm-but-not-cute microcopy. UK-licensed, profitable on a lending book. **Wins:** the
  emotional fingerprint of *instant, exact, human* notifications; best-in-class everyday transaction clarity.
  **Leaks:** UK-centric; some features feel playful in a way a premium European brand wouldn't. *gökberk's
  counter:* steal the instant-spend feel (patterns.md §10), drop the whimsy for editorial restraint.
- **Starling** — the quietly excellent operator. UK, profitable early, marketplace integrations, strong
  business banking, multiple "Best British Bank" awards. **Wins:** reliability, breadth done soberly, trust.
  **Leaks:** less consumer-flashy; UK-only. *gökberk's counter:* the same sober reliability, with a more
  premium, pan-European finish.
- **Chime** — US, no-fee + early-payday. ~22M users; won on fee-free overdraft and getting people their
  payroll two days early — i.e. solving a *real cash-flow pain* to earn the primary relationship. **Wins:**
  a sharp wedge into primary-account status. **Leaks:** US-only model, thin product depth. *Lesson for
  gökberk:* a single feature that solves a real pain (cash flow, certainty) can win the relationship better
  than ten novelty features.
- **Nubank** — the scale champion. ~125M users in Latin America by serving the underbanked with zero
  bureaucracy, smart credit scoring, and a clean app; expanded into insurance and investments. Highly
  profitable (>$2B net income) on a credit book. **Wins:** simplicity at massive scale, credit-led depth.
  *Lesson for gökberk:* depth (credit, the next product) is where the money and the relationship are — but
  it's earned by trust and simplicity first.

## The cross-border specialist

- **Wise** — the transparency gold standard. Unmatched for international transfers: true mid-market FX rate
  shown openly, fee broken out, recipient-gets amount up front, believable ETA, live tracking, local account
  details in ~10 currencies. **Wins:** radical transparency as the entire product. **Leaks:** narrow — a
  transfer and multi-currency tool, not a full bank. *gökberk's counter:* if any FX/SWIFT surface is less
  transparent than Wise, it's wrong. Wise sets the disclosure bar; gökberk must meet it inside a fuller bank.

## The invest-led / wealth challengers (the "Nordnet depth" axis)

- **Trade Republic** — mobile-first European neobroker turned bank. ~€150B client assets, cheap securities
  trading, ETF savings plans, fractional shares, and a high-interest cash account that pulls deposits in.
  Revenue from transaction fees, securities lending, PFOF (declining), and net interest. **Wins:** dead-simple
  investing for a mass audience; the savings-plan habit. **Leaks:** thin on full-bank features; PFOF optics.
- **Bitpanda** — Austrian, crypto-first broker that expanded into ~8,000 stocks, ~2,500 ETFs, and 650+
  cryptocurrencies. Spread-based pricing (~1.49% standard). **Wins:** one app for crypto + traditional
  assets, broad asset menu. **Leaks:** spread model is less transparent than commission; crypto-heritage
  trust questions. *Lesson for gökberk:* multi-asset in one place is attractive, but be more transparent on
  cost than the spread model.
- **Nordnet** — the Nordic digital wealth powerhouse. ~2.2M customers, deep brokerage + pensions + savings;
  balanced revenue across trading commissions, net interest, and fund fees that offset each other across
  cycles. **Wins:** *depth* — real research, instruments, pension wrappers, the serious investor's tool.
  **Leaks:** less of a daily-spending bank. *gökberk's axis:* the spec frame is literally "Revolut feel,
  **Nordnet depth**, traditional-bank breadth" — Nordnet is the bar for the investing surfaces.

## The payments / BNPL-turned-bank

- **Klarna** — BNPL pioneer now a digital bank. ~118M users; Pay Now / Pay Later / installments plus a
  growing banking layer. The instructive metric: "banking consumers" (those using more than payments)
  generate ~$107 revenue/user vs. ~$30 for payments-only users. **Wins:** a colossal funnel and the proof
  that *depth multiplies value per user 3×*. **Leaks:** BNPL's debt-encouragement reputation cuts against a
  trustworthy premium brand. *Lesson for gökberk:* depth pays, but choose products that build trust, not
  ones that monetize impulse.

## The incumbents (the contrast class)

Traditional retail banks are the foil gökberk defines itself against: clunky multi-step transfers, hidden FX
margins, standing-order/direct-debit confusion, poor pending visibility, dead-end errors, and dated UIs — but
they still hold the salary, the deposits, the mortgage, and the cross-sell. **gökberk wins** by being clearer,
faster, and calmer *while* matching their breadth and earning the same primary relationship — neobank
experience, incumbent depth and trust.

## What "next-gen" means for gökberk bank

Not "more features than Revolut." Next-gen here is:

- **Transparency as the trust signal** — Wise-grade disclosure (rate, margin, fee, ETA) before every commit,
  everywhere, in plain numerals.
- **Calm over noise** — the right option surfaced, the rest tucked away; restraint where the field is loud.
- **The instant-spend fingerprint** — fast, exact, trustworthy, reversible-where-possible (patterns.md §10).
- **Breadth without bloat** — accounts, payments, cards, lending, investing, insurance under one calm roof,
  each held to a world-class standard, none half-built.
- **Earning the primary relationship** — solving real pains (clarity, certainty, control) so this becomes the
  account people run their life from, which is where depth, lifetime value, and the business actually live.

Your competitive verdict is always: *meets table-stakes flawlessly + beats the field on the axes gökberk
chose to win (clarity, trust, calm, transparency, depth), without chasing surface area for its own sake.*
