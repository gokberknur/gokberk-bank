# Product principles — what gökberk bank is trying to be

The north star and the principles you measure every decision against. The brand is editorial restraint —
monochrome canvas, one earned forest-green accent, calm typography, hairline-and-flat surfaces, quiet motion,
a restrained voice. These principles are that same ethos applied to **product**, not just pixels: what we
choose to build, how much, and to what standard. When a verdict needs grounding in *what this product is*,
this is the source.

## North star

> **Be the bank people trust enough to run their life from.**

Not the spare travel card, not the second account the salary never reaches — the **primary relationship**.
Everything is judged on whether it moves us toward that: more trust, more daily habit, more depth (the next
product held), fewer surprises. A feature that doesn't plausibly serve the primary relationship is activity,
not progress (see `value-framework.md` for the metrics).

The product ambition, in the spec's own words: *"Revolut feel, Nordnet depth, traditional-bank breadth"* —
the experience of the best neobank, the seriousness of a real wealth platform, the completeness of an
incumbent, all under one calm roof.

## The principles

### 1. Quality over quantity — restraint is the product

Ten features at a world-class standard beat thirty half-built ones. The premium feeling comes from *what we
chose not to do* as much as what we did. We would rather ship fewer surfaces, each one you'd screenshot, than
match a competitor's feature count and feel cluttered doing it. Surface area is a permanent cost; spend it
like it's scarce, because it is.

### 2. Trust is the foundation, not a feature

People only move their real money to a bank they trust to be boring and correct. So trust outranks novelty,
always. Concretely: accurate balances, honest pending states (never fake completion), every fee and rate
disclosed *before* commit, no-blame error copy, reversibility where possible, calm under pressure. We never
monetize against the customer's interest — no dark patterns, no impulse-debt mechanics, no attention farming.
The calm, transparent posture is a *growth strategy*: trust is the precondition for depth.

### 3. Transparency as the signature

Where the field hides FX margins and buries fees, we disclose — rate, margin, fee, ETA, who-sees-what — in
plain numerals, before the user commits. Wise-grade transparency, everywhere, inside a fuller bank. The
differentiator isn't a new rail; it's a transaction you understand *completely* before you make it.

### 4. Calm over noise

The right option surfaced, the rest tucked under "More." One earned accent on the primary action. No confetti,
no gamified nags, no ten entry points where one would do. Where Revolut is dense and loud, we are calm and
clear. Calm is not the absence of capability — it's capability *organized* so the user is never overwhelmed.
Every new tab, card, and quick-action is taxed against this.

### 5. Premium means finished, not flashy

Premium is restraint and craft, not gloss. A premium surface is one where the empty state is considered, the
pending state is honest, the error is kind, the numbers are tabular and aligned, and nothing is half-built.
The "Metal-tier" feeling comes from completeness and polish, not from animation or ornament.

### 6. Depth over breadth, when forced to choose

The money and the relationship live in depth — the next product held, the salary that lands, the investment
account that grows — not in another shallow island. When choosing, deepen an existing surface to excellence
before widening into a new one. Breadth that adds islands nobody returns to is bloat wearing an ambition
costume.

### 7. Every screen is one bank

The app must feel like a single premium institution, not a pile of features by different teams. Cross-cutting
patterns (the money spine: review → confirm → success; the wizard; step-up; notifications; the instant-spend
fingerprint — see `.planning/ux/patterns.md`) are reused everywhere so the whole coheres. A feature that
needs its own bespoke pattern owes an explanation.

### 8. Solve real pains to earn the relationship

The features that win the primary account solve a *real* pain — cash-flow certainty, clarity, control — not a
manufactured want. Chime won payroll-early; Wise won transparency; Monzo won everyday clarity. One feature
that removes a genuine anxiety beats ten that add novelty. Ask of every proposal: *what real fear or friction
does this remove?*

## How these become a verdict

When you judge a feature, these principles are the *standard* the value framework measures against:

- A proposal that fights restraint, trust, transparency, or calm is **off-brand** → a Cut or a Reshape,
  regardless of how clever it is.
- A proposal that deepens the primary relationship and solves a real pain, at a quality that honors the
  premium bar, is the kind of thing we **Ship**.
- "Good enough to ship" means: clears the table-stakes flawlessly, honors these principles, and meets the
  design system's and the domain expert's bar — not "it works."

The brand promise and the product promise are the same promise: a calm, transparent, trustworthy, premium
bank that earns the right to be the one you run your life from. Hold every decision to it.
