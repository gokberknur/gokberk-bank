# PFM & rewards — competitive benchmarks

How the best do money management and loyalty, so gökberk bank can match or beat them. Use this to calibrate
"how good does this have to be?" — the answer is usually "as clear as Monzo Trends, as honest as the best,
and calmer than all of them."

## The bar-setters — PFM

- **Monzo (Trends & Targets)** — the gold standard for **everyday spend clarity**. Week-by-week and
  month-by-month spending by category, "left to spend" framing, and Targets that *suggest achievable,
  personalised budgets from your past spending* rather than scolding you. Trends folds in connected accounts
  for a full picture. Microcopy is warm, human, never preachy. If our budgets surface is less clear or more
  judgmental than Trends, it's wrong.
- **Emma** — the **subscription detector and aggregation** benchmark. Emma's whole pitch is visibility across
  every account plus *finding recurring subscriptions and flagging the ones you no longer need*. In-depth
  analytics, detailed budgets. The lesson: the subscription detector is a headline feature, not a footnote —
  it's what users credit with saving them money.
- **Revolut (Analytics)** — fast, dense spend analytics, category breakdowns, budgets, and rich charts.
  Powerful but busy. Match the depth; stay far calmer and quieter.
- **Snoop / Plum** — proactive monitoring: price-hike alerts, duplicate-charge detection, "okay to spend"
  projections. The lesson: the valuable nudge is the *factual, money-saving* one — a price rise, a duplicate
  — not a motivational poster.

## The bar-setters — rewards

- **Klarna** — stores rewards in its own balance, nudging spend back into the ecosystem. The lesson is a
  **warning**: ecosystem lock-in and "spend it here" pressure is the manipulative end we avoid — our cashback
  credits a real wallet the customer already controls.
- **Revolut (rewards)** — personalised, location-based offers, points, challenges, even crypto cashback,
  with leaderboard-style comparison. Genuinely engaging — and exactly where the gamification line gets
  crossed. Take the *personalised-offer relevance*; **leave** the challenges, leaderboards, and streaks.
- **Cashback programmes (card-linked offers)** — activate-an-offer, spend, earn, redeem, all in-app. The good
  ones disclose the cap and expiry plainly; the bad ones bait with "up to X%". Be the former.

## Patterns worth stealing

- **Monzo Targets' "derived from your own spending"** — budgets *suggested* from real history, framed as
  achievable, never as a rebuke.
- **Emma's subscription detection** — recurring charge → cadence → next date → "do you still need this?" with
  a cancel path. Headline it.
- **Snoop's factual money-saving alerts** — price hikes, duplicate charges. The only nudges worth pushing.
- **"Left to spend" / projected end-of-month** — useful *if* honest and clearly an estimate.
- **Card-linked offers done plainly** — one clear reward, cap and expiry visible, redeem to a real balance.

## Anti-patterns to avoid (where even the leaders slip)

- **Vanity charts** — a beautiful donut nobody can act on; analytics for their own sake.
- **Shaming insight** — "You spent too much on X", red walls, guilt framing. Never.
- **Gamified overspending** — streaks, leaderboards, challenges, confetti, spend-more-to-unlock; engagement
  metrics dressed up as wellbeing.
- **Reward bait** — "up to 10%", hidden caps, surprise expiry, points worth nothing, ecosystem lock-in.
- **A category total that disagrees with the ledger** — the trust-killer; one parallel store and it's over.
- **Rainbow category fills** — twelve saturated colours; noise pretending to be information.

## The gökberk angle

Match Monzo on clarity and Emma on subscription-hunting; **beat** them all on calm, honesty, and respect.
Where Revolut gamifies, we inform. Where Klarna locks value in, we credit a real wallet. One earned
forest-green accent (the focused category, the single primary action, the one featured offer); the neutral
ramp for everything else — never a chart rainbow, never a red wall. The differentiator isn't a flashier
chart or a bigger reward; it's a money picture you completely trust and a thank-you with no strings.
