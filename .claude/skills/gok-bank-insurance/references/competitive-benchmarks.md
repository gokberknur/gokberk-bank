# Insurance — competitive benchmarks

How the best present protection, so gökberk bank can match or beat them. Use this to calibrate "how good does
this have to be?" — the answer is usually "as honest as the best, calmer than all of them."

## The bar-setters

- **Lemonade** — the gold standard for **claims UX**. File in-app in seconds, instant claim number to track,
  no phone menus or hold times; simple claims auto-decided, complex ones routed to a human who reaches out.
  The lesson to steal: claims as a *fast, transparent, low-friction* process with a visible status — minus
  their gamified "instant payout" theatre. Our claims are honest about pacing (review is real), not
  performative.
- **Revolut (insurance)** — the **embedded/bancassurance** benchmark: device, travel, purchase, and
  pay-per-day cover offered *inside the banking app*, at the point of need, paid from the wallet. The lesson:
  relevant cover in context, frictionless buy, managed alongside money. Match the embedded ease; stay calmer
  and more transparent about exclusions than a fast upsell tends to be.
- **Qover / embedded insurers** — the **API-embedded** model and its UX thesis: embed protection *at the point
  of need*, treat regulation as an opportunity for transparency (not an excuse for friction), and run a
  fintech-style test-and-learn to remove claim friction. The lesson: insurance should feel native to the host
  journey, not bolted on.
- **Bancassurance (traditional)** — the contrast and the cautionary tale: the bank already holds your data, so
  the quote is fast — but historically the cover was opaque, exclusions buried in a PDF, and the cancel path a
  retention maze. We win by being the *honest* bancassurer: same data-driven speed, none of the opacity.

## Patterns worth stealing

- **Lemonade's in-app claim + live tracker** with a reference and a clear current stage.
- **Revolut's point-of-need embedded offer** — cover surfaced in context, bought from the wallet in a couple
  of taps.
- **The IPID's "what is / what is not insured" side-by-side** — the regulatory artefact that already does
  equal-weight cover/exclusions; make it interactive.
- **Transparent quote breakdown** — premium, excess, add-on deltas itemised before commit (the Wise-grade
  transparency lesson, applied to protection).
- **A believable "decided within N days"** expectation on a pending claim, instead of a fake instant verdict.

## Anti-patterns to avoid (where even good insurers fail)

- **Burying exclusions** — smaller type, a closed accordion, or only in a linked PDF. The cardinal sin; see
  `references/cover-vs-exclusions.md`.
- **Upsell-led quotes** that shout the cover and whisper the limits to lift conversion.
- **Retention dark patterns** on cancel — guilt copy, hidden buttons, "are you *sure* you want to lose
  protection?" loops.
- **Faking an instant claim decision** when a human review is actually pending — erodes trust on the first
  real claim.
- **Blaming claims copy** — "your claim was rejected", "you failed to…", treating a customer like a suspect.
- **Cover-date and excess ambiguity** — vague periods, an excess the customer only meets at claim time.

## The gökberk angle

Match the embedded insurers on point-of-need ease and Lemonade on claims clarity; **beat** all of them on
honesty and calm. Where a fast upsell whispers the exclusions, we show them at equal weight. Where a
traditional bancassurer buries the IPID, we make it the centre of the quote. The differentiator isn't a
cheaper premium or a flashier claim animation — it's that you finish the quote understanding *exactly* what
you are and aren't buying, and you file a claim feeling like a customer, not a suspect. Transparency is the
product.
